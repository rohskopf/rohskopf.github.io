Modeling Interatomic and Intermolecular Interactions.

This involves method development to more easily model interactions in any molecule/material. Such models are rooted in quantum mechanics, and often take the form of machine learning and/or complex optimization problems.
  • This thrust is central to most others, as properly modeling interactions between atoms is paramount to modeling processes in terms of these interactions.
  • Investigating general procedures/methods to machine learn interatomic potentials that accurately predict thermal and mechanical properties of solids and liquids, especially those used in energy engineering (semiconductors, refrigerants, etc.).
  • How can we probe and measure these interactions experimentally?

Computational Materials/Chemical Discovery.

With the advent of fast supercomputers and GPUs, machine learning approaches to discovery have become more tractable.
  • Molecular dynamics models of disordered solids and interfaces to optimize thermal transport and mechanical properties.
  • Kinetic theory models of low density fluids to optimize transport properties.

Molecular Biophysics and Proteins.

I'm particularly interested in the physics of cell metabolism; this is a phenomenal nanoscale energy conversion process which we could learn a lot from in engineering our own nanoscale systems, and in the study of disease pathways that may originate in these processes (e.g., the electron transport chain). Goals here include:
  • Deeper insights into how biological molecules/proteins interact (especially those involved in cancer/disease pathways), and how to influence these interactions chemically.
  • Understand the fundamental physics of interactions between biomolecules and proteins. This involves crafting interatomic potentials via quantum mechanics and machine learning, which I have spent a lot of time on for solid materials.

Multiscale Modelling.

Designing accurate models that traverse vast length and time scales will provide more insight into various processes of technological interest (e.g. materials processing, shockwaves, explosions, etc.)
  • Develop methods to model energy transport from nano to micro scales.

Sustainability.

Design optimizations for heating and cooling systems to reduce energy costs.
  • Develop regional or environmental specific optimizations for building efficiency to save energy/resources.

Energy Conversion, Storage and Generation.

  • Investigate novel methods of energy storage/generation.

Manufacturing and Materials Processing.

Enormous amounts of energy are used daily to manufacture goods and process materials. How can we better optimize such processes?

Techno-economic Models.

Interfacing physics/engineering calculations with economic models.
